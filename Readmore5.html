Description

Batanes is a group of islands located in the northernmost part of the Philippines, bordered by the Luzon Strait to the west and the Pacific Ocean to the east. The islands are known for their stunning landscapes, rugged coastlines, and unique culture. The Ivatan people, the native inhabitants of Batanes, have a distinct culture and way of life that sets them apart from the rest of the Philippines. They have a strong sense of community, and their traditional houses, called "vakul", are made of cogon grass and serve as protection from the harsh weather conditions of the region.



Culture


"vakul"

The Ivatan culture is characterized by a strong sense of community and social harmony, and the people have a deep respect for their natural environment. The traditional Ivatan houses, known as "vakul," are made of cogon grass and are designed to withstand the harsh weather conditions of the region, including typhoons and earthquakes.
