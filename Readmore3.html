Description

PThe island of Boracay is only 7 km long and 1 km wide, but it boasts over a dozen beaches, each with its own unique character and charm. The most famous of these beaches is White Beach, which is known for its powdery white sand and turquoise waters. Other popular beaches on the island include Puka Beach, Bulabog Beach, and Diniwid Beach. In addition to its beautiful beaches, Boracay is also known for its vibrant nightlife. The island has a wide range of bars, restaurants, and clubs that cater to both local and international visitors. There are also numerous water sports activities available on the island, such as scuba diving, snorkeling, and parasailing.



Culture


"Aklanon Culture"

TAklan’s famous Ati-Atihan Festival is a feast held every January in honor of Santo Niño, showcasing tribal dance and music, accompanied by indigenous ensembles, and a vibrant parade. It is regarded as one of the best festivals in the Philippines, as well as the country’s version of Mardi Gras.


"Exciting Water Sports Activities"

Adventure has no age limit in Boracay. Ride the waves in style, feel the wind in your face, and try something new. For adrenaline junkies, the island offers different activities that will satisfy your craving for something exciting. Stand up paddle on the beach, go kite surfing, snorkeling, and scuba diving. You can also have the greatest time with your family and friends by trying fun water activities such as riding a banana boat and UFO, and helmet diving. If you want to go extreme, you can do parasailing where you will be treated with a birds-eye-view of the whole island from above while being pulled by a boat.
