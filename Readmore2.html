Description

Bacolod is a city located in the western part of the island of Negros in the Philippines. It is the capital of the province of Negros Occidental and is known as the "City of Smiles" because of the friendly and hospitable nature of its people. Bacolod is also known for its food, particularly chicken inasal, a grilled chicken dish that is popular throughout the Philippines. The city has a rich cultural heritage and is home to various festivals such as the MassKara Festival, which is celebrated every October and features colorful masks and costumes. Additionally, Bacolod is a center of education, commerce, and healthcare in the region.



Culture


"MassKara Festival"

The MassKara Festival was first celebrated in 1980 as a way to uplift the spirits of the people of Bacolod, who were going through a period of economic hardship. The festival is named after the words "mass" which means many, and "kara" which means face. The festival is a celebration of the Bacolodnons' resilience and their ability to smile in the face of adversity. During the festival, the streets of Bacolod are filled with dancers wearing colorful masks and costumes. The dancers perform to upbeat music, and the festival is marked by street parties, food fairs, and parades. One of the highlights of the festival is the MassKara Queen Beauty Pageant, which showcases the beauty and talent of the women of Bacolod.


"San Sebastian Cathedral"

TThe San Sebastian Cathedral was built in the 19th century and is known for its beautiful architecture, which features a mix of Gothic and Renaissance styles. The cathedral is named after Saint Sebastian, who is the patron saint of Bacolod. The interior of the cathedral is equally impressive, with beautiful stained glass windows, ornate altars, and intricate carvings. The cathedral also has a large pipe organ, which is used during Mass and other religious services.
