Description

Bohol is an island province located in the Central Visayas region of the Philippines. The culture of Bohol is a blend of indigenous traditions, Spanish colonial influences, and modern Filipino culture. One of the most prominent cultural attractions in Bohol is the Chocolate Hills, a geological formation of more than 1,200 hills that turn brown during the dry season, resembling chocolate mounds. The hills are considered a national geological monument and are a popular tourist destination in the province. The indigenous people of Bohol are known as the Boholanos. They have a rich culture that is heavily influenced by their relationship with the natural environment. Many of their cultural traditions revolve around agriculture and fishing, which are still an essential part of the local economy.



Culture


"The Sandugo festival"

The indigenous people of Bohol are known as the Boholanos and have their unique traditions and customs that are still practiced to this day. One of their most popular cultural practices is the Sandugo or blood compact, a ritual that involves drinking wine mixed with the blood of both parties to signify a bond of friendship or alliance. The Sandugo festival is an annual celebration of this event and features street dancing, parades, and cultural performances.


"Loboc Church"

Bohol is also home to some of the most beautiful and historical churches in the country. The Baclayon Church, which is one of the oldest stone churches in the Philippines, and the Loboc Church are both designated as National Cultural Treasures. These churches showcase the Spanish colonial influence on the province's culture and architecture.
